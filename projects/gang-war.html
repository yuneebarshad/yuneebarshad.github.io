Overview

ROLE: Senior Game Developer — gameplay architecture, progression & monetization

Gang Mafia Shooting Crime Game is a mobile third-person action shooter centered on gang territory control. Players create their gang, fight through rival-controlled city areas, complete objective-driven combat missions, recruit gang members, upgrade weapons, defeat bosses, and capture new territory through a map-based progression system. I built the game's full system layer on top of the Invector third-person controller framework — combat tuning, mission and territory progression, weapon and recruitment systems, the in-game economy, save persistence, and mobile monetization — and prepared the project for iOS release alongside its Android launch. The game shipped on Android; its iOS listing was released and has since been suspended.

Gameplay Loop

Select mission→ Fight enemy waves→ Complete objective→ Earn XP & cash→ Upgrade & recruit→ Capture territory

Key Contributions

  • Built the game's full system layer on top of the Invector third-person framework, including combat tuning, aiming, shooting, reload logic, and ragdoll hit reactions.
  • Designed a territory-based progression system across 3 rival gangs, with named locations, boss levels, and locked/unlocked area states.
  • Implemented multiple mission types — attack waves, survival objectives, capture-point missions, boss fights, and vehicle-style gameplay — rather than one repeated mission structure.
  • Built a weapon system covering multiple gun categories with individual damage, magazine size, reload time, fire rate, and upgrade costs.
  • Designed a gang member recruitment system with named, recruitable characters, each with distinct stats and level-based unlock requirements.
  • Added JSON and PlayerPrefs-based save systems covering levels, weapons, gang members, economy, and onboarding progress.
  • Used Unity Addressables to dynamically load UI screens — map, gun selection, pause menu, level complete, boss eliminated, and capture success/failure.
  • Integrated AppLovin MAX mediation for banner, interstitial, rewarded, and app-open ads, alongside Firebase Analytics for ad impression and revenue tracking.
  • Prepared and shipped an iOS build (version 1.1.4) alongside the Android release; the iOS listing is currently suspended.

Core Systems

✓ Combat System (Script)

Built on top of the Invector third-person controller framework, tuned and extended for this game's specific combat feel.

  • Player aiming, shooting, reload logic, and ammo handling, with weapon switching across multiple gun types
  • Camera/FOV shake and slow-motion tuning layered on top of the base Invector controller
  • Ragdoll hit reactions on enemies for physical, responsive combat feedback
✓ Territory & Mission Progression (Script)

A map-based structure where progress is measured in territory captured, not just levels cleared.

  • Three rival gang territories, each with named locations and a distinct boss level
  • Locked/unlocked area states tracked per territory, gating progression as areas are captured
  • Mission rewards and capture states tied directly into the overall progression structure
✓ Mission Variety (Script)

Multiple distinct mission structures rather than one repeated combat loop reskinned across levels.

  • Attack-wave missions and survival objectives with escalating enemy pressure
  • Capture-point missions with win/loss states tied to holding territory
  • Boss fights and vehicle-style missions for structural variety across the territory map
✓ Weapon System (Script)

A named, statted weapon roster rather than a single generic "damage number" per gun.

  • Multiple gun categories — SMGs, assault rifles, pistols, and shotguns — each with individual damage, magazine size, reload time, and fire rate
  • Per-weapon upgrade costs and progression, tied into the game's overall economy
✓ Gang Member Recruitment (Script)

Recruitable characters with their own identity and stats, not a single interchangeable "hire crew" button.

  • Named recruitable gang members, each with distinct damage and accuracy stats
  • Level-gated unlock requirements per recruit, with persistent recruit state saved across sessions
✓ Save & Persistence (Script)

Keeps territory progress, weapon upgrades, and recruited gang members intact across sessions.

  • JSON-based save data covering completed, locked, and unlocked levels per territory
  • PlayerPrefs-based persistence for weapons, gang members, economy, and onboarding state
✓ Addressables UI Loading (Script)

Dynamic UI loading rather than baking every screen into a single upfront build.

  • Unity Addressables used to load map, gun selection, and pause menu screens on demand
  • Level complete, boss eliminated, and capture success/failure screens loaded the same way, keeping the UI layer decoupled from the core build
✓ Monetization & Analytics (Third-Party Integration)

Production ad monetization paired with revenue tracking, not just ads bolted on without visibility into performance.

  • AppLovin MAX mediation across banner, interstitial, rewarded, and app-open ad formats
  • Firebase Analytics connected for ad impression and revenue event tracking
